Frank Brockmann

Computational Media Researcher

Investigating how conversational AI mediates identity formation, cultural sovereignty, and care infrastructure during critical life transitions

Research Focus

How do computational systems reshape human practices of care, learning, and identity formation? My work examines the algorithmic mediation of critical human experiences—from parents seeking crisis support to indigenous communities protecting linguistic sovereignty.

Core Interests

  • Conversational AI & Identity: How AI dialogue systems scaffold self-understanding during life transitions
  • Algorithmic Mediation of Care: What changes when support infrastructure becomes computational
  • Cultural Sovereignty in AI Systems: Designing AI that resists colonial patterns of knowledge extraction
  • Platform Affordances & Human Agency: How computational infrastructure shapes possible actions and experiences
